Stuck on a Cliff!

On a cold October night, I embarked on a hunt with my dad and my grandpa. Grandpa was driving and insisted that we use his truck to ascend the mountain rather than the ATV. As we began our climb in his old rusty dodge, we came to a sharp bend in the road. The truck’s front tires made the turn effortlessly but the back tires came inches from cutting the corner. From there, the road only became more treacherous. We came to the conclusion that it would be better to return to the base of the mountain for the night and climb up the mountain on dad’s ATV the next day.

As we came to that familiar kink in the road grandpa made a critical error. Before we knew it, the left side of the truck was high-centered on the edge of a 300-foot cliff. We came to a stop and dad quickly pulled grandpa and me to the right side of the truck to keep our weight from pulling the vehicle off the cliff. We tried to use a towing cable to escape but the weight of the truck was just too much. We tried everything imaginable to get the truck back to solid ground, but it was no use. Using the last of the towing cable, dad tied the truck up to a wimpy tree nearby. With nothing left to do but wait for someone else to arrive, we made the daunting decision to sleep in the truck overnight. We shivered all night long as a cold winter wind beat against the truck.

That night was one of the longest nights of my life. Occasionally dad would ask me to climb to the other side to turn on the car for heat as I was the lightest at the time. I’ll never forget how grateful I was the next day to hear the sound of several ATVs approaching our truck. Miraculously, with the aid of other hunters, we were able to save the truck and continue our hunt. To this day, I still consider that night to be my craziest hunting adventure.

Beaver Creek

As a child, I spent most of my summers at Bear Lake hunting, boating, spending time with cousins, and fishing. So naturally, when I met my wife-to-be I was eager to introduce her to the wonders of Garden City, Utah. On her first visit to our family cabin, I showed her Chubby’s Hollow, near to where I had shot my first buck, and gave her a tour of all of our other favorite honey holes. But I was most excited to take her out fishing.

My uncle’s fishing boat that’s at the cabin wasn’t working so we decided to explore Beaver Creek. I had luck fishing this river before, but never like I did that spring with Heather. The river was flowing with fish! We didn’t have a way to tie the fish up so we built an impromptu pond for our fish. Soon enough, our small dam was filled with our daily limit. Heather hadn’t fished much since her childhood and she was thrilled to have had so much success. Personally, I was just happy to have the greatest catch of all fishing beside me.
